Meet Our Masters

Master Kenji Takara

Master Kenji Takara, founder of Dobu, has dedicated over 35 years to martial arts. Now in his early fifties, he is revered for his wisdom and the depth of his knowledge in both traditional and modern martial arts. His journey began in rural Japan, where he was trained by his father, a respected martial artist. From a young age, he learned that true strength comes from balance, humility, and respect. Master Takara believes in merging discipline with compassion, teaching students to honor both their own strengths and those of their opponents. His teachings emphasize mental resilience as much as physical skill, guiding students to develop a holistic approach to martial arts that enriches all areas of life.

Senior Master Hiroshi Tanaka

Sensei Hiroshi Tanaka, Dobu's senior master, has been a part of the dojo since his twenties. Now in his early forties, he is known for his patience and precise techniques, which have earned him deep respect among students and peers alike. Growing up in Okinawa, Sensei Tanaka was inspired by his community’s deep-rooted martial arts traditions, where he first witnessed the transformative power of discipline and focus. His training combines a strong foundation in both judo and aikido, enabling him to develop a unique style that emphasizes fluidity and control. Sensei Tanaka is dedicated to helping students cultivate not only physical skills but also mental resilience, empowering them to build inner strength, balance, and adaptability. He believes in teaching martial arts as a way of life, guiding students to carry these values into their everyday experiences.

Senior Master Mei Ling

Sensei Mei Ling, one of Dobu's leading instructors, brings over a decade of experience in martial arts, with a focus on self-defense and empowerment for all ages. Originally from Vietnam, she learned the value of discipline early, studying under renowned Southeast Asian masters who instilled in her a deep respect for tradition and perseverance. Now in her thirties, she champions mental focus and agility, aiming to inspire confidence and strength in each of her students. Sensei Mei is known for her ability to create a supportive and encouraging environment, where students of all levels feel motivated to challenge themselves. She emphasizes the importance of self-belief and resilience, teaching that true empowerment comes from within. Through her guidance, students not only learn valuable skills but also develop a sense of pride and inner peace that extends beyond the dojo.

Senior Master Yu Ka

Sifu Yu Ka is a senior instructor, known for his strong grounding in martial arts, with a particular focus on traditional Lethwei. Now in his early thirties, he integrates these roots with modern techniques, providing students with a holistic and dynamic training experience. Deeply passionate about teaching, Sifu Yu Ka emphasizes practical skills that are both effective and rooted in cultural tradition. He believes that understanding the historical and cultural background of martial arts enriches the training, helping students connect their practice to a deeper sense of purpose and heritage. His classes are known for their intensity and depth, inspiring students to embrace martial arts as a journey of personal growth, resilience, and respect for tradition.

Junior Master Leo Tran

Renshi Leo Tran, a junior master in his mid-twenties, brings a vibrant energy and sense of innovation to the Dobu dojo. Originally from Thailand, he has trained in Muay Thai since a young age, drawing on its powerful techniques to create dynamic and engaging classes. Renshi Tran’s teaching emphasizes flexibility, reaction time, and a fierce fighting spirit, inspiring his students to embrace challenges with confidence and focus. Known for his encouraging yet demanding style, he pushes students to test their limits, helping them build both physical and mental resilience. Renshi Tran believes that martial arts should foster courage and self-assurance, empowering students to carry these qualities into all aspects of their lives.

Junior Master Evan Haung

Junior Instructor Evan Haung, the youngest in the Dobu team at 20, is dedicated to mastering and teaching various martial arts styles. A native of South Korea, his background is rooted in Taekwondo, which he began practicing at the age of five under the guidance of local masters. He brings a fresh perspective and unwavering dedication to Dobu, inspiring students with his enthusiasm and skill. Especially passionate about teaching younger students, Instructor Haung focuses on instilling core values such as discipline, self-respect, and mental fortitude. Known for his approachable and encouraging style, he strives to create a positive learning environment where students feel supported in their journey. His goal is to show students that martial arts can be a powerful tool for personal growth, resilience, and lifelong confidence.

Our History

Dobu was founded with a vision to merge the timeless traditions of martial arts with the needs of modern practitioners. At its core is Master Kenji Takara, whose journey began decades ago in rural Japan, where he trained under his father. From a young age, Master Takara embraced not only martial techniques but a philosophy of balance, discipline, and respect.

Initially a small dojo for a few devoted students, Dobu quickly gained a reputation for blending traditional practices with a focus on mental resilience and personal growth. Word spread, and students from across regions came to learn, drawn to Dobu as a place of both physical and spiritual development.

As Dobu grew, it remained a sanctuary for those seeking community and authenticity in an era of commercialized martial arts. Master Takara’s values of humility, respect, and inner strength resonated with his students, shaping Dobu’s identity as a path to personal growth.

Over the years, new instructors joined, enriching Dobu’s curriculum with their expertise in disciplines like judo, aikido, Lethwei, and Muay Thai. Together, they created a well-rounded environment that maintained Dobu’s philosophy.

Today, Dobu thrives as a dojo that combines tradition and modernity, led by Master Takara and his dedicated team. Dobu’s mission is not just to teach techniques, but to instill resilience, respect, and a lifelong pursuit of learning, making it more than a dojo — it’s a legacy.

Our Philosophy

The philosophy of Dobu is deeply rooted in the balance between tradition and self-discipline, guiding practitioners to develop both their physical skills and their inner strength. At Dobu, we believe that martial arts is not merely a series of techniques but a journey that shapes one's character and spirit. Inspired by the graceful yet powerful image of the Red Crane, Dobu embodies principles of elegance, resilience, and harmony. Our approach encourages students to embrace challenges, build resilience, and cultivate respect for others. Dobu sees martial arts as a way to connect with history and cultural heritage, allowing practitioners to walk a path that has been paved by generations of dedication and honor.

In every class, students learn that true strength goes beyond physical ability; it lies in the discipline to train consistently, the humility to learn from others, and the patience to progress at one’s own pace. Dobu’s philosophy places great emphasis on the values of respect, self-awareness, and personal growth, guiding students to become not only skilled in martial arts but also more mindful and compassionate individuals. We foster a supportive community where each member encourages others on their journey, believing that martial arts should build not only individual power but also collective bonds of respect and friendship. This philosophy reflects our vision of creating a space where the ancient traditions of martial arts can evolve alongside modern principles of inclusivity and personal development, empowering each student to reach their full potential, both within the dojo and in their everyday lives.
